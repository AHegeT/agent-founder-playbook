# Playbook: The 3-Phase Networking System

One of the biggest bottlenecks for startups is finding the right people. Be it finding talent, your first customers, or the right investors, knowing the right person can be critical.

What we've learned is that most critical relationships must be nourished, and trust needs to be built. This is a system for moving beyond just collecting contacts and into a structured process for building relationships, based on our debrief of Mexico Tech Week 2025.

---

## Phase 1: Preparation (Pre-Event)

Success at an event is often determined by the preparation done beforehand. This phase is about clear-eyed research and setting a mission.

- **Define Your Objective:** What is the primary, realistic goal for this event? (e.g., "Meet 3 founders in the EdTech space," "Get 1-2 warm intros to pre-seed investors.")
    
- **Conduct Research:** Identify key speakers, sponsors, and attendees. For your "Top 10" list, use AI tools to summarize their recent posts, interviews, or company news. This provides context for genuine conversation.
    
- **Prepare Your Tools:**
    
    - **Note-Taking System:** Have your personal CRM (like Dex or Clay) or a simple note-taking app ready.
        
    - **Follow-Up Template:** Draft a simple, 2-line follow-up message. You will personalize this later, but having a template removes friction.
        

## Phase 2: Engagement (During-Event)

This phase is about being present, listening, and capturing information. This is the human-centric part of the system that cannot be automated.

- **Be Present and Genuine:** Your primary goal during a conversation is to listen and understand the other person's context.
    
- **The Post-Conversation Note:** This is the most critical step. Immediately after a conversation, step aside and log 3 points in your CRM or note app:
    
    1. **The Person:** Name and company.
        
    2. **The Context:** Where/how you met (e.g., "At the Angel Ventures talk").
        
    3. **The "Key Detail":** One specific, human detail from the conversation (e.g., "Just moved from [City]," "Is looking for a [Role]," "Loves [Hobby]"). This is essential for the follow-up.
        
- **Connect:** Exchange LinkedIn, email, or your preferred contact method.

## Phase 3: Follow-Up & Nurturing (The Relationship Phase)

This is where the real value of networking is built. This system helps you move from a "nice to meet you" email to a strategic, long-term relationship.

### Step 1: The "First Touch" Follow-Up (Within 1-5 Days)

Your first follow-up is not an "ask." It's a simple, human "handshake" that moves the connection from in-person to digital. People are busy, so giving yourself this 5-day window makes the process more realistic and sustainable.

- **Action:** Send a simple, non-transactional follow-up message.
    
- **Goal:** To confirm the connection, reference your conversation, and ensure you're in their inbox.
    
- **Template:** "Hi [Name], great to meet you at [The Context]. I really enjoyed our chat about [Key Detail]. Hope to stay in touch."
    

### Step 2: The Triage (Categorize Your Contact)

This is the most critical strategic step. After they are in your CRM or contact list, you must decide _what_ this relationship is. This dictates your _next_ action.

#### Tag 1: Immediate Priority

- **Who:** A direct potential investor, key customer, or "dream hire" for a specific, active role.
    
- **Goal:** Move to a focused, "deeper dive" conversation (this could be a virtual coffee, a 20-minute call, etc.).
    
- **Next Action:** A week or two after your "First Touch," send a clear, respectful "ask" for a focused conversation.
    

#### Tag 2: Key Relationship

- **Who:** High-value peers, potential mentors, or future-state investors/customers. There is no _immediate_ ask, but you want to build a long-term, high-trust relationship.
    
- **Goal:** To build trust and establish yourself as a helpful, intelligent peer.
    
- **Next Action:** Add them to a "Key Relationship" list. Your follow-up will be purpose-driven, using the "Meaningful Touchpoint Menu" (see Step 3).
    

#### Tag 3: Helpful Connection

- **Who:** People in your industry where the most immediate value is in your _shared_ networks (your "introduction" and "back pocket" group).
    
- **Goal:** To be a helpful "node" in the network. The easiest way to build trust here is to _give_ help first.
    
- **Next Action:** Look for a "5-Minute Favor" you can do, like making a helpful introduction (see Step 3).
    

#### Tag 4: General Network

- **Who:** Everyone else. Good people, in your industry, but with no immediate, clear connection.
    
- **Goal:** To be a "familiar face" in their network.
    
- **Next Action:** No active nurture is needed beyond connecting on LinkedIn. A simple "congrats" on a work anniversary or new job is sufficient.
    

### Step 3: The "Meaningful Touchpoint" Menu

This is your "how-to" guide for nurturing your **Key Relationship** and **Helpful Connection** groups. Instead of a random reminder, you use one of these _purpose-driven_ plays when the time is right.

#### Menu Item: The "Double Opt-In" Introduction

- **What it is:** Connecting two people in your network who would benefit from knowing each other.
    
- **How:** "Hi [Name], I was just speaking with [Person X], who is working on [Problem]. It made me think of you. Would you be open to me making a (no-pressure) introduction?"
    
- **Why it works:** It's selfless, helpful, and positions you as a valuable connector.
    

#### Menu Item: The "I Saw This and Thought of You"

- **What it is:** Sharing a highly relevant, specific piece of information (an article, a report, a podcast) that relates _directly_ to your "Key Detail" note.
    
- **How:** "Hi [Name], I just read this article on [Topic]. It reminded me _exactly_ of our conversation about [Problem]. Thought you might find it interesting."
    
- **Why it works:** It shows you were listening and that you understand their context.
    

#### Menu Item: The "Smart, Specific Question"

- **What it is:** A respectful, low-lift way to get advice that honors their expertise.
    
- **How:** "Hi [Name], my team is starting to tackle [Problem X]. I remember you mentioned you... [did Y]. What's the _one_ resource you'd recommend, or the _one_ mistake you see people make?"
    
- **Why it works:** It's not a "Can I pick your brain?" (a burden). It's a specific, flattering, and easy-to-answer request.
    

#### Menu Item: Celebrate the Win

- **What it is:** The simplest, most effective touchpoint.
    
- **How:** "Saw on LinkedIn that [Company] just [launched X / got funded / was featured in Y]. A huge congratulations to you and the team!"
    
- **Why it works:** It's 100% positive, requires no reply, and shows you are paying attention.

## 🧰 Our Community Toolkit

Here are some of the favorite resources and tools our community members use to build and nurture professional relationships.

### 1. For Your Bookshelf (The Core Philosophy)
A few key books that shape our "gardener, not hunter" approach to networking.

* **Give and Take** by Adam Grant
    * **Why we recommend it:** This is the core philosophy. It's a data-backed guide on why a helpful, "Giver" approach is more effective and sustainable than a transactional one. It's the "why" behind **Phase 3**.
* **Never Eat Alone** by Keith Ferrazzi
    * **Why we recommend it:** This is the tactical playbook. It's full of practical, repeatable systems for building relationships, especially for the "Pre-Event" and "Post-Event" phases.
* **How to Win Friends and Influence People** by Dale Carnegie
    * **Why we recommend it:** A classic for a reason. It’s the foundational guide on the *human* side of the "During-Event" phase: being genuinely interested, remembering details, and making people feel heard.

### 2. For Your Feed (People We're Learning From)
A few of the thought leaders our community follows for ongoing insights on building better professional relationships.

* **Adam Grant**
    * **Why we follow:** His work (podcasts, articles) is a constant source of inspiration on building positive-sum, helpful relationships.
* **Dorie Clark**
    * **Why we follow:** She's an expert on personal branding and "playing the long game," which is key for building "Key Relationships" that last.
* **Keith Ferrazzi**
    * **Why we follow:** For practical, in-the-trenches advice on keeping your networking system running effectively.

### 3. For Your Workflow (The "How-To" Tools)
The tools our members use to run their networking systems, from simple and free to more advanced.

* **The "Starter" System: Google Sheets / Airtable**
    * **Why we use it:** The perfect first "Personal CRM." It's free, 100% customizable, and forces you to *build your system* (like your **Phase 3 Triage** tags) before you pay for a tool.
* **The "Research" Tools: LinkedIn Premium & Perplexity AI**
    * **Why we use them:** This is your **Phase 1** research stack. LinkedIn is for finding *who* to connect with. Perplexity is for understanding *why*—it can "read" their recent articles or interviews to help you find a genuine, shared interest.
* **The "Relationship" CRMs: Clay & Dex**
    * **Why we use them:** If your Google Sheet is overflowing, these tools are built for this. They are designed for the **Phase 3** "Nurture" process, making it much easier to remember key details, set reminders, and manage your "Meaningful Touchpoints."
